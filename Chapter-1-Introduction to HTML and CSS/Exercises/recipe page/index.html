Mutton Mandi

Mutton Mandi is a traditional Arabian dish featuring tender, slow-cooked mutton with fragrant rice and a blend of aromatic spices. This dish is known for its smoky flavor and melt-in-your-mouth meat.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg mutton (with bones, preferably shoulder or leg)
  • 3 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 5-6 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp mandi spice mix (or equal parts black pepper, cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon)
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 4 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 3 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1 tbsp salt (adjust to taste)
  • 5 cups water (for cooking rice)
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves for garnish
  • Fried onions for garnish (optional)

Method

  1. Marinate the mutton with yogurt, half the mandi spices, turmeric, cumin, garlic, and salt for at least 2 hours (overnight for best results).
  2. In a large pot, heat 2 tbsp ghee and oil. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add the marinated mutton and sear on high heat for 5 minutes until browned.
  4. Reduce heat, cover, and cook on low heat for 1.5-2 hours until meat is tender (add water if needed).
  5. In another pot, heat remaining ghee. Drain soaked rice and add to the pot with remaining mandi spices. Sauté for 2 minutes.
  6. Add 5 cups water and salt. Bring to boil, then reduce heat and cook covered until rice is 70% done (about 10 minutes).
  7. Layer the partially cooked rice over the cooked mutton in the first pot. Cover with tight lid.
  8. Cook on very low heat for 20-25 minutes (dum style) until rice is fully cooked and flavors combine.
  9. For authentic flavor, place a piece of burning charcoal in a small bowl on top of the rice, drizzle with oil, and cover for 5 minutes.
  10. Garnish with fresh herbs and fried onions before serving hot with salad and yogurt sauce.
